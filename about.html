About Us

Zoological Survey of India (ZSI), with more than 100 years history of documenting and disseminating knowledge on the faunal richness of India, has an indispensable role to play in enlightening the scientific fraternity/academicians and the general public with updated information on the faunal diversity of India. ZSI is working tirelessly to explore the mysteries of faunal diversity of India.

The best and most promising way of disseminating such information is to generate easily accessible, user friendly and updatable digital platforms enriched with information on diversity and distribution of faunal taxa of India along with their detailed illustrations and taxonomy through World Wide Websites.

Under the dynamic leadership of Dr. Dhriti Banerjee, Director, Zoological Survey of India, the first of its kind website with voluminous information on the diversity and distribution of Indian Lepidoptera is dedicated to the scientific fraternity as well as to the general public. The website will provide a common platform to the scientific fraternity/ academicians of India as well as of the world, Environmentalists, Agriculturists, Forest forces, amateur naturalist, for sharing scientific knowledge on the diversity of Indian Lepidoptera.

The website is being handled by Lepidoptera Section, ZSI, Kolkata, which is primarily involved in collection, preservation, identification, cataloguing, computerization, digitization and documentation of Lepidopteran (moths and butterflies) fauna of our country. The section holds more than 1, 15, 000 specimens of Indian Lepidoptera. There are about 4000 species and subspecies of butterflies (including from outside India) and more than 2500 species and subspecies of moths housed in the section. The Lepidoptera section houses collection from 49 countries and more than 750 type specimens. The first registered specimen of Lepidoptera section is a male butterfly, Hasora umbrina Mab., collected by N. E. Celebes and was catalogued by Cap. Evans on October 01, 1931. The collections of de. Niceville, K. Jordan, W.H. Evans, E. E. Green, G. C. Dudgeon are some of the historical collections housed in the Lepidoptera section.
